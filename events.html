The Trombone Night

Every last Saturday of the Month the bar hosts the Trombone Night in honour of the bars first female mixologist, Lady Tasha Trombone, an avid fan of the same instrument. The night boasts of both local jazz musician acts. It is an acoustic perfomance of various jazz instruments but predominantly The Trombone. It is a celebration of the strides made in the classic artistry that is jazz. The last Night, was headlined by the popular Jazz maestro Duke Ellington.


The Jacques Festival

The Jacques Festival is regarded as one of Europe's most prestigious jazz festivals, bringing a touch of the European scene to the UK and jazzing it up with the country's famed camping culture. But Jacques' isn't all about jazz, with its lineup spanning the worlds of funk, soul and blues too. 2019's lineup will feature sets from Snarky Puppy, Jamie Cullum and Gladys Knight. Beyond the music, festival-goers will also have the chance to immerse themselves in the festival's other side, such as its diverse variety of workshops, wellbeing classes, vintage markets, incredible food and drink stalls, and its kids area, perfect for the young Jacques'.


Industry Night

One hand still poised on the keys of her saxophone, Lisa Rosenkrantz turns her back to the crowd to count off to her band in a near-whisper: “One, two, three, four.” When she turns back, she is cast in a greenish hue from the lighting. She bends her knees, and with her eyes closed begins a soulful melody. This is "Jazz on Thursday" at The Industry. Rosenkrantz’s band, the Lisa Rose Jazz Quartet, has alternated playing with the Hennessy Brothers Jazz Quartet each week in this intimate bar since the series began in February. The two groups take on recognizable jazz standards with a twist, such as Duke Ellington’s “Caravan.”
